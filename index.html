Why Every VC Firm Sounds the Same — And Four Ways to Fix It

An original research note on the differentiation problem in venture capital marketing. Written from the inside of a VC firm that was trying to solve it.

"Every funding announcement starts with 'We are proud to invest in… extraordinary founder / extraordinary team…' — no defined brand language, no original voice."

The Three Brand Archetypes

VC firms lean on one or a mix of three archetypes to establish their brand. Knowing which one you are is the starting point for any differentiation strategy.

Institutional

Credibility → The Firm
  • Legacy & Esteem (Sequoia, Accel)
  • Thesis Specialist (Speciale Invest)
  • Hyper-Local (Blume)
  • Impact-Focused (Lightrock)

Key Man

Credibility → The Individual
  • Operator-VC (Titan Capital)
  • Contrarian (Founders Fund)
  • Creator-Led (The Generalist)

Portfolio Proof

Credibility → Performance
  • Business Proof (unicorns, IPOs)
  • People Proof (testimonials)

Four Levers to Stand Out

01

Narrative Style

The hero's journey is dead. What works: founder depth (failures and decisions, not milestones), people-first stories (early hires, culture builders), and thematic storytelling across multiple voices — not a linear journey.

02

Content Format

Short-form Q&A works for early-stage. Long-form (2,500+ words) is the real gap in India — no VC firm is doing it, and it's the format that signals genuine care, access, and depth. It also becomes a single source of truth for media.

03

Naming Convention

Builder / Scale / Brunch / Playbook — these words are exhausted. What works are metaphor-driven names that build their own world: Flight School, Inside the Engine Room, Pitstop. Visual names create memorable identities.

04

Design Language

Most VC firms — even the top ones — don't get design right. The opportunity is a distinct visual identity for each content property so everything feels intentional and recognisable, not just loosely on-brand.
